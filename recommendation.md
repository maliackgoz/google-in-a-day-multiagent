Over the next phases, this single-node MVP would evolve into a production-ready search stack by hardening crawl and index paths, then scaling horizontally. On the crawl side, that means a durable URL frontier (for example backed by a log or message bus), per-domain politeness, pools of stateless fetch workers, and explicit robots and allowlist policy. Indexing would move toward a streaming pipeline: fetch events through parse, normalize, and enrich stages into sharded, optionally replicated inverted segments, with clear separation between write-heavy ingestion and read-heavy query serving. Persistence would become first-class—crawl state, document metadata, and index segments in reliable storage—with health checks, metrics, alerting, and structured logging for 24/7 operation.

Once ingestion and serving can scale out, the focus shifts to ranking quality and cloud-native packaging. Lexical retrieval (BM25-style or better) would feed candidate URLs to optional rerankers (link signals, clicks, learning-to-rank). Stateless query frontends behind a load balancer would fan out to sharded index replicas with caching and failover. Container images, infrastructure-as-code, tenancy, and rate limits would allow shared clusters to grow safely. The multi-agent development workflow used to build this MVP can still apply at the process level: separate human or AI “owners” for crawl SLOs, index correctness, and serving latency, each with review gates before production rollout.
